FAQ

EV charger installation questions, answered

How much does EV charger installation cost in Sydney?
Most standard home installs, where the charger is mounted near the switchboard or garage, fall within a predictable fixed-price range. The main variables are cable distance, switchboard condition and single vs three-phase supply. Send us photos and we'll give you an exact fixed price before you commit to anything.
How long does the installation take?
A typical home EV charger installation takes three to five hours. If your switchboard needs an upgrade first, we'll flag that in the quote and usually complete both in a single visit.
Do I need three-phase power to charge an EV at home?
No. A single-phase 7kW charger fully recharges most EVs overnight and suits the majority of Sydney homes. If you have three-phase supply, a 22kW-capable charger is an option, and we'll tell you honestly whether your car can actually use the extra speed before you pay for it.
Will my switchboard need an upgrade?
Many older Sydney homes have boards with no spare capacity or outdated protection. We assess this from your photos before quoting. If an upgrade is needed, it's priced up front, and it improves the safety of your whole home, not just the charger circuit.
Can you install a charger I've already bought?
Yes. We install customer-supplied chargers, including Tesla Wall Connectors bought direct from Tesla, provided they're compliant for use in Australia. We'll confirm compatibility from the model number before we book you in.
Do you do apartments and strata buildings?
Yes. We handle owner-corporation approvals paperwork, metering options and load management for shared supply, from a single bay install to a building-wide charging plan.
